Trend

play-driving components by season
OFF RAPM — how many extra scoring chances (expected goals per 60 min) this player's team CREATES because he's on the ice, after adjusting for who he plays with and against. +0.30 means his presence adds about a third of a goal of offense per hour. League average is 0.
DEF RAPM — same idea for the other end: how much he SUPPRESSES the other team's chances. Positive = makes his team harder to score on.
REL xG% — the share of scoring chances his team gets with him ON the ice minus the share WITHOUT him. +5% means the team controls play five points better when he's out there. The simplest "does the game tilt the right way with him on?" number.
All three are 5v5 and xG-based; single seasons wobble — read multi-season direction, not one dot.

Comparable players

same position, same age, most similar career shape

Similarity matches the career THROUGH this age — WAR trajectory, role, off/def split, skill shape, size. The line under each name says why: the two axes where the pair sits closest, and the biggest gap when it tops 0.8 standard deviations — the match is explainable, not a black box. "Next 3" is what each comp actually did over his following three seasons: a tendency, not a destiny — the spread is the message. Backtested: the similarity-weighted comps projection predicts next-3 WAR at r≈0.62, ahead of both carry-forward and the aging curve (≈0.50); lockout and COVID seasons are pro-rated to 82 games. Size and Speed are disclosure, not matching: speed is EDGE tracking (2021-22 onward — older comps show a dash, and weighting it tested WORSE for prediction). A 40th-percentile skater matching a 99th-percentile skater's arc is doing the work a different way — that's worth seeing, not hiding.
